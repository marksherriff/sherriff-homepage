## Candidate's Most Significant Professional Accomplishments

Over more than twenty years in computing education, I have made sustained national contributions to computing education through leadership in the ACM SIGCSE community, the development of mentoring and research infrastructure for computing education researchers, and innovative curricular leadership at the University of Virginia.

A major focus of my academic work has been strengthening the computing education research (CER) community through mentoring, professional development, and the promotion of rigorous empirical methods. Through multiple National Science Foundation (NSF) funded projects, I created and sustained opportunities to support empirical CS education projects and researchers. I co-authored an updated work on the current standards of reporting for empirical studies in CS education venues. At the same time, I co-created created the Designing Empirical Education Research Studies (DEERS) workshops and related mentoring initiatives that supported computing educators as they developed and conducted their own empirical research studies. These efforts have provided structured mentorship, research training, and collaborative opportunities for educators and emerging researchers from institutions across the United States. 

My DEERS colleagues and I were awarded a new grant from the NSF in January 2026 for a project called REPLICATE with the purpose of directly supporting replication in CS education research and creating new frameworks to support replication work in the future. My work has directly contributed to improving the rigor, reproducibility, and accessibility of computing education research while helping expand participation in the research community itself.

Another significant contribution has been my leadership in the NSF Showcase program at the ACM SIGCSE Technical Symposium. Through multiple NSF-funded grants, I helped build and sustain a venue that allowed computing education researchers and educators to share developing projects, receive formative feedback, connect with collaborators, and engage directly with NSF program officers. The Showcase has become an established and valued component of the SIGCSE community and has supported the dissemination and growth of computing education innovation nationwide.

I have also played a sustained leadership role in the ACM SIGCSE community. My service includes election as Secretary of the ACM SIGCSE Board (2025-2028) and membership on the SIGCSE Technical Symposium Steering Committee. I served as Symposium Co-Chair for the 2020 and 2021 ACM Technical Symposium on Computer Science Education, helping guide the conference community during the disruptions caused by the COVID-19 pandemic and supporting the transition to large-scale online conference operations. Once the SIGCSE Technical Symposium was back in-person in 2022, I continued to host SIGCSE Coffee Break, an online interview and commentary program designed to support virtual attendees and strengthen community engagement during the symposium. Recordings of my show can be found at [http://sigcsecoffeebreak.org](http://sigcsecoffeebreak.org). I have additionally served in numerous organizational and program leadership roles for SIGCSE and related conferences, including Associate Program Chair, workshop organizer, reviewer, and virtual event host.

At the University of Virginia, I have demonstrated long-term leadership in curriculum innovation and scalable computing education. I helped redesign our foundation curriculum, serve as associate chair for undergraduate programs, and developed large-enrollment software engineering and computing courses that have impacted thousands of students. My teaching and educational leadership have been recognized through numerous honors, including the IEEE Computer Society Mary Kenneth Keller Computer Science and Engineering Undergraduate Teaching Award and the University of Virginia All-University Teaching Award. In recognition of his sustained contributions to student education and development, I was named the Joseph L. Vaughan Professor of Humanities, an endowed professorship recognizing sustained contributions to student education and development.

Across these activities, my contributions reflect a consistent commitment to advancing computing education not only through teaching and scholarship, but also through the creation of professional communities, mentoring structures, and organizational infrastructure that support the long-term growth of the field. These mentoring and workshop efforts have helped broaden participation in computing education research by supporting educators from a wide range of institutions and career stages.
