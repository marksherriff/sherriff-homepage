In previous offerings of CS 4730, everyone was assigned the same game (or small set of games) to play during the semester. Recent examples have included **A Short Hike**, **Portal**, **Celeste**, and **Eastshade**.

This semester, we're taking a different approach.

Instead of requiring everyone to play the same games, you'll choose your own path through a curated library of **20 games** spanning more than forty years of game design. The games are divided into two categories:

* **Foundational Design** – Games that established or fundamentally changed the way we think about game design.
* **Contemporary Design** – More recent games that build on, reinterpret, or challenge those foundational ideas.

Rather than completing every game, each has a **challenge milestone**. These milestones were selected because they represent the point where the game's primary design lesson should be clearly visible without requiring a full playthrough.

During the semester, every student will complete:

* **One challenge from the Foundational Design list**
* **One challenge from the Contemporary Design list**

Students who complete additional challenges will unlock a few extra opportunities during the semester, but there is no expectation that everyone will finish all twenty.

## Getting the Games

Don't let cost keep you from choosing a game.

For many of the Foundational Design titles, I have handheld emulation devices available for checkout, or you're welcome to use your own legally obtained copies or emulation setup. I also own multiple copies of several games on Steam and expect to have a small number of Steam Decks available to lend when appropriate.

In short, **you should be able to complete both required challenges for anywhere from free to about $10**, depending on the games you choose. For example, you could borrow my copy of **Super Metroid** on an emulation device and purchase **A Short Hike** for only a few dollars during a sale.
